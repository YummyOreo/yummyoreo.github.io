Hi! I'm a highschool student and developer who also loves the history of medicine. To stay active I am the captain of my high school ultimate frisbee team (who will hopefully do better this year!). I sometimes post notes and blog posts about topics I'm interested in.


I got into coding just before the pandemic with web dev. I was inspired by the various games out there and tried to make some myself. After going down the rabbit hole of coding (speed up by COVID) I tried to make my own things. All of them looking back are horribly coded, but if you want to look, you can find them on my Github if you scroll far enough.


Recovering from the COVID pandemic and lockdowns, I found an online community called TBFighters, which I (hesitantly) joined and decided to put my webdev skills to use and help them code their website. Talking with the amazing people behind TBFighters, I gained in interest in the history of medicine and started to research that. I currently work on the TBFighters website (improving it to the best of my abilities) and dive into the history of medicine.
